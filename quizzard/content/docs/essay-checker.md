The essay checker is a focused AI review pass for a text page. Instead of chatting with Scholar Chat, you point it at a draft and get structured feedback on what's working and what's not.

## What it checks

The reviewer looks at your draft across several dimensions:

- **Grammar and mechanics** — spelling, punctuation, subject-verb agreement, tense.
- **Clarity** — sentences that are hard to parse, ambiguous pronouns, buried verbs.
- **Structure** — whether the argument flows, whether paragraphs have a clear job.
- **Tone** — whether the register fits what you're trying to write (formal essay vs. casual reflection).

## How to use it

1. Open the text page with your draft.
2. Launch the essay checker.
3. The AI reads the whole page and returns feedback inline.
4. You decide what to accept — nothing is rewritten without your say-so.

It's meant as a second pair of eyes, not an autopilot. The goal is to flag problems so you can fix them, not to replace your writing.

## When to use essay checker vs. inline AI

- **Essay checker** — whole-page review. Use when you're ready for feedback on a full draft.
- **[Inline AI](/docs/inline-ai)** — selection-scoped rewrite / summarise / expand. Use when you want to fix a specific sentence or paragraph in place.
